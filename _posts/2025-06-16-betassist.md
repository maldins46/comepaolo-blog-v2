"What's the project you're most proud of?" Every software developer has faced this question at least once. For me, the answer has stayed the same for years: **BetAssist**.

## The Beginning

It was 2017, and I was deep into my first year of university. Like a lot of Italian students, I was obsessed with football — not just watching it, but understanding it. Formations, statistics, predictions. I'd spend hours on different websites cross-referencing data before matches.

One day it hit me: *why am I doing this manually?*

## Building the App

BetAssist started as a simple Android app. The idea was straightforward: aggregate live scores, match statistics, and form data into a single, clean interface. No clutter, no ads plastered everywhere — just the data you need, presented well.

I wrote it in Java, back when Kotlin was still finding its footing in the Android world. The architecture was... let's call it "enthusiastic." I was learning as I went, making mistakes, refactoring, and learning some more.

## What Made It Special

The thing that made BetAssist different wasn't the technology — it was the obsession with detail. Every screen was designed to answer a specific question. Every animation had a purpose. I treated it like a product, not a side project.

At its peak, the app had a small but dedicated user base. People actually used it. They sent feedback, reported bugs, requested features. For a university student building his first real app, that felt incredible.

---

BetAssist taught me more about software development than any course ever did. It taught me about users, about persistence, and about the pride that comes from building something real.

*The app is no longer maintained, but the code — and the lessons — live on.*
