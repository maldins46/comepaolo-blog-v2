Summer, 2018 — Jesi. It was one of those summers when time feels endless. I had just wrapped up my internship and was halfway through writing my bachelor's thesis. But the thing I remember most vividly isn't the code I wrote or the papers I read. It's the basketball.

## How It Started

It began the way most good things do: casually. A few friends, a half-court, and a shared love for the game. 3x3 basketball had just started gaining traction internationally — it was set to debut at the Tokyo Olympics — and we thought, *why not bring it to Jesi?*

We called it **Canestreet 3x3**, a play on *canestro* (the Italian word for basket) and street. The idea was simple: organize a summer tournament in the heart of the city, open to everyone, with good music and good vibes.

## The First Edition

That first summer, we didn't know what to expect. We printed flyers, posted on social media, and hoped for the best. Sixteen teams showed up. Sixteen! For a tournament organized by a handful of twenty-somethings with zero budget and a borrowed sound system.

The games were intense. The crowd was electric. And by the end of the night, we knew we had something special.

## Growing Year After Year

Each summer brought more teams, more spectators, and more ambition. We partnered with local businesses for sponsorships, got proper equipment, and even attracted players from neighboring cities.

But the heart of Canestreet was always the same: community. It was about bringing people together, celebrating sport, and proving that you don't need a fancy arena to create something meaningful.
